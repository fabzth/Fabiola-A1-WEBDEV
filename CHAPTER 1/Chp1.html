MATCHA TIRAMISU 抹茶ティラミス

Here’s the delightful recipe for matcha tiramisu. We hope you prepare it with lots of LOVE!


INGREDIENTS

  • 3 Egg yolks
  • 70g Granulated sugar
  • 225g Mascarpone cheese
  • 350g Whipping cream
  • Matcha powder
  • Vanilla extract
  • Water (hot)
  • Ladyfinger biscuits

How to Make Matcha Tiramisu

For the Matcha Mascarpone Cream

  1. Prepare a double boiler. Bring 1" of water to a boil, whisk the egg yolks and granulated sugar in a mixing bowl, then place the bowl over the pot to create a double boiler.
  2. Heat the egg mixture. Continuously whisk until the sugar dissolves and the mixture becomes pale in color, which should take about 5 minutes.
  3. Remove the bowl from the heat and carefully incorporate the softened mascarpone cheese.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the whipping cream, matcha powder, and vanilla extract. Use an electric mixer with a whisk attachment to whip until medium peaks form. The cream should hold its shape but remain fluid.
  5. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until combined.

For the Matcha Soak

  1. In a shallow dish, mix matcha powder, granulated sugar, and hot water. Whisk until slightly frothy, which should take about 30 seconds.
  2. Dip each ladyfinger into the matcha soak, ensuring both sides are coated, then allow any excess to drip off. Avoid soaking them too long to prevent them from becoming mushy.

Assembling the Matcha Tiramisu

  1. Arrange the ladyfingers at the bottom of your serving container.
  2. Add a generous layer of mascarpone cream on top of the ladyfingers and smooth it out evenly.
  3. Repeat the layering process with another round of soaked ladyfingers followed by a layer of mascarpone cream.
  4. Cover the tiramisu and chill it in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and the ladyfingers to soften.
  5. Before serving, gently dust with matcha powder.

Storage

  • Your matcha tiramisu can be stored covered in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. It can also be prepared 1-2 days in advance and stored until ready to serve.

Freezing

  • This matcha tiramisu can be frozen in an airtight container for up to 1 month. Just thaw it in the refrigerator overnight before serving.
